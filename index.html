I am a PhD student in Computer Science at the University of Illinois Chicago, advised by Dr. Zhiling Lan and Dr. Michael Papka. My work sits at the intersection of high-performance computing, GPU systems, data science, and scientific workflows, with current interests in reproducible evaluation, anomaly detection, and computational tools for high-energy physics.

C2 the P2 Funding

My graduate work is supported by C² the P², the Chicagoland Computational Traineeship in High Energy Particle Physics. The program trains physics and computer science students in computational tools for particle physics through coursework, seminars, lab collaborations, retreats, and research integration with partners including UIC, NIU, Argonne, and Fermilab.
